So, you’re sat in your flat, hungover, with a 2-hour lecture ahead of you. It is a tough situation, however before you contemplate skipping the lecture so you can lounge about in a groggy state, there are some University rules and regulations regarding attendance that you should know.
The following sums up the official rules and regulations of Newcastle University that can be found on the University’s page about absence.

  1. Students are expected to turn up to all lectures, seminars etc. If you cannot attend any element of your course, you should submit a Student Notification of Absence Form.
  2. If you have any difficulties or queries concerning absence, you should contact your tutor and he/she should be able to support you.
  3. If you miss anything, it is your responsibility to catch up! Blackboard can be used to view the notes that you missed, or you can use Re-Cap to go through the whole lecture.
  4. Assume, your lectures will be on during standard term time. For example, if you have heard a strike will occur during University hours, you should be contacted by your lecturer or someone from your course to inform you if your lecture is cancelled. Don’t just assume it is.
  5. Attendance in lectures is recorded by either a sign-in sheet, which you physically have to sign, or by scanning your university card into a scanner which should be provided.
  6. If you submit a Student Notification of Absence Form, don’t always assume it will be authorised. The situation at the top of the screen for example may not be taken too seriously!
  7. If you are an international student, because of Home Office rules, you are expected to attend at least 80% of your classes.

There are some other rules regarding post-graduate study that once again can be found on the University’s page about absence.

REMEMBER: You have chosen to be here, and are paying a significant amount of money for your course. If you still struggle to make those 9 o’clocks, then maybe that will stir you!
